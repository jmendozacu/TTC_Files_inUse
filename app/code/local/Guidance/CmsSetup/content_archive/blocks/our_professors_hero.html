  • The audience for the Great Courses is much wider and more interesting than that commonly found in a college classroom. The pressure to make material engaging to a wide audience, while simultaneously up to the standards of many discriminating students, often leads one to structure and present topics more cleverly, more clearly, and more effectively than one has before.

    Patrick Grim Ph.D.
    Boston University. State University of New York at Stony Brook
