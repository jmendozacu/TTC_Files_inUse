Downloading FAQ

Getting Ready to Download

What should I do before downloading courses or installing the Course Downloader?
Before downloading courses or installing the Course Downloader:
  • Turn off or disable your browser’s pop-up blockers
  • Turn off firewalls and security software
  • Make sure you have administrative privileges on the computer you’re using
  • Make sure you have enough free space on your hard drive
What are the system requirements?
We recommend a broadband connection for downloading. If you do not have a broadband connection, please download one lecture at a time to avoid interruptions.

What systems or devices can I download to?
The Great Courses can be played on almost any device that has a media player. Currently, you cannot download directly to most devices such as iPad or iPhone, but you can copy your course lectures to your computer, then to your device. Some steps to follow to do this:
  • Download your lectures to a computer
  • Import your files into your media player, such as iTunes. You may have to open each file separately to import properly.
  • Sync your device (for example, iPad, iPhone or tablet) with your computer as you normally would.

Downloading Files

How do I download my courses?
After the Course Downloader is installed on your computer, the courses you have purchased will be available in My Downloads in the My Account section of our site. If you do not see your downloadable courses, then sign out, close your browser and sign back in.
How long does it take to download?
On average, using a broadband connection, each lecture takes approximately 10-15 minutes to download. We recommend downloading no more than three lectures at a time.
What if I have trouble downloading?
If you have trouble downloading, try the following:
  • Turn off or disable your browser's pop-up blockers
  • Turn off firewalls and security software
  • Make sure that you have administrative privileges on the computer you're using
  • Make sure you have enough free space on your hard drive
  • Use a different browser to download.
I'm getting a 404 error (file not found) on a specific lecture. How do I clear my download cache?
If you get a file not found error on a specific lecture, this is likely an issue with the cache for the Akamai client, rather than your specific web browser.
  • PC users:
    To clear your cache, go to your start menu for your operating system. Click on the control panel, then open Akamai Netsession Interface Control Panel. Click on preferences, then the clear button in the download cache folder.
  • Mac users:
    To clear your cache, go to your system preferences (located under the Apple logo, top left on your screen). Then select “other options” from the bottom left of the window, click on Akamai. Select preferences, then the clear button in the download cache folder.
What media formats are available?
AUDIO FORMATS
Our audio courses are offered in two download formats:
  • M4B is recommended for iPod and iTunes users who want bookmarking, which is the ability to keep track of where you last left off listening.
  • MP3 is recommended for all other systems and devices. This includes PCs, Mac computers, and non-iPod personal listening devices.
VIDEO FORMATS
Our video courses are offered in two download formats:
  • M4V is recommended for iPod and iTunes users who want bookmarking, which is the ability to keep track of where you last left off listening.
  • WMV is recommended for all other systems and devices. This includes PCs, Mac computers, and non-iPod personal listening devices.
Can I download a course more than once?
Yes, you can download course lectures as many times as you want. Your downloads will be available in My Downloads in the My Account section of our site.
Can I load my downloads on multiple systems and devices?
Yes, you can download or copy files onto multiple systems and devices. However, before downloading, please make sure that you have selected the right media format for the system or device. See "What media formats are available?" in this section.
How do I find my downloads on my computer?
When you start the download, you will be prompted to select the folder to which you'd like to save your courses. We recommend creating a folder on your desktop called "The Great Courses". You can create subfolders in this folder to help you organize your courses (see topic under Saving and Organizing Files). If you can't find your courses, then you can search your computer for the course number or check where downloaded files typically go. Some possible locations are:
  • On a PC: C:\..[username]\My Documents\Downloads
  • On a Mac: ...[username]\ Downloads. You can check this by going to your browser and choosing Preferences. In the General tab, look at the setting "Save Downloaded Files To".
How do I download my course guidebooks?
Your course guidebooks will be available in .pdf format in My Downloads in the My Account section of our site. You will need Adobe Acrobat Reader to view these files. You can download a free copy from Adobe's site.
